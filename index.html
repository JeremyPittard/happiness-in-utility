ITCSS

Traditionally we would try to build this with something like a combination of ITCSS and the BEM naming convention. If we look at the diagram on the screen, the idea behind ITCSS is you organise your classes into these 7 categories, and the further down the triangle you get, the more specific the classes are.
ending up with something like...
this.
Which is highly effective for small teams efficient with css and who are also familiar with this sort of file structure. **however** it is extremely time consuming. You need to set up your variables/design tokens, your folder structure, then break it down granually and set up appropriate css/scss imports. Then the real challenge, maintiainging it. Preventing it from becoming Spaghetti code, writing the documentation and keeping it up to date.
its also fragile, as your system grows and your team changes you gather tech debt, it's invevitable, the adding things here and there to meet a deadline with every intention to clean it up later.
What if we could just plug your design tokens into a config, and voila! ready to go? Enter, Tailwind.
